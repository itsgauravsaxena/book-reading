# Privacy Policy — bibliophil

_Last updated: 16 June 2026_

bibliophil ("the app") is a personal reading tracker. We designed it to keep your
data on your device. This policy explains what the app does and does not collect.

## Data we collect

**None.** bibliophil does not have user accounts, and we do not operate a server
that receives your reading data. Everything you enter — your books, reading
sessions, goals, and imported history — is stored locally on your device using
Apple's Core Data, and (for the home-screen widget) in a private App Group
container on the same device. We have no access to it.

We do not use analytics, advertising, tracking, or crash-reporting SDKs.

## Information sent to third parties

When you **search for a book** by title, author, or ISBN, the text of your search
query is sent to one or both of these services to return matching titles and
cover images:

- **Google Books API** — https://policies.google.com/privacy
- **Open Library API** (Internet Archive) — https://archive.org/about/terms.php

These requests contain only your search text. They are not associated with any
account or identifier we create, because the app has none. If you never search,
the app makes no network requests.

## Data sharing and sale

We do not collect your data, so we do not share or sell it.

## Children's privacy

The app does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone,
including children under 13.

## Your control over your data

Because your data lives on your device, you can remove all of it at any time by
deleting the app. You can also delete individual books and sessions inside the
app.

## Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at the URL where you
found it, with a revised "Last updated" date.
